**2. A Free and Highly Literary Translation:**

**CHAPTER V.**

...it seemeth foolish that one should not enjoy those pleasures that carry no reproach, and that one should turn away from the gracious gifts of nature through injustice.

**9** But thou, to my mind, dost seem to act yet more foolishly, if, vainglorious concerning the truth, thou wilt still mock me with thine own punishment.

**10** Wilt thou not wake from thy senseless philosophy?

**11** And wilt thou cast away the folly of thy reasonings, and taking to thyself a mind worthy of thine age, wilt thou philosophize the truth of what is profitable?

**12** And, having shown reverence for my humane counsel, wilt thou not pity thine own old age?

**13** For consider ye, even if there be some overseeing power of this thy religion, it will surely pardon thee for every transgression done through compulsion.

**14** In this wise, the tyrant did exhort [Eleazar] unto the unlawful eating of flesh.

**15** Then Eleazar sought leave to speak. And having received authority to speak, he began to declaim thus:

**16** "We, O Antiochus, being persuaded to live by divine law, do deem no compulsion more violent than that toward [which] we deem worthy to pasture.

**18** And yet, even if in truth our Law were not divine, as thou dost suppose (but otherwise we deem it to be divine),

**19** neither in this wise was it [for] us to annul the díxan concerning piety.

**20** Deem not this a small sin, therefore, if we should eat defiling foods.

**21** For to transgress the Law in small things and in great things is equivalent;

**22** for through each, the Law is equally scorned.

**23** And thou dost mock our philosophy, as if we did not live therein with sound reason.

**24** For temperance doth teach us, so that we may master all pleasures and desires,

**25** and doth exercise courage, so that we may willingly endure all hardships;

**26** And righteousness doth instruct [us], so that through all morals we may teach equality [before the law];

**27** And piety doth teach [us], so that we may reverently worship the only existing God with magnificence.

**28** Therefore, we do not eat defiling foods. For believing that the Law is established by God,

**29** we know that He hath permitted us to eat those things which shall be suitable unto our souls,

**26** but hath forbidden us to eat those things which shall be contrary.

**27** Moreover, it is tyrannical, not only to compel us to transgress the Law,

**27** but also to eat [these things], that thou mightest yet mock us with this our most hateful defiling of food.

**28** But thou shalt not mock me with this laughter;

**28** neither shall I abandon the sacred testicles of our ancestors concerning the keeping of the Law."
